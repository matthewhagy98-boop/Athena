Executive Summary

This study investigates the prefrontal cortex activation patterns during complex decision-making tasks in high-frequency trading simulations. By employing high-resolution fMRI and EEG synchronization, the researchers identified a distinct neural "bottleneck" that occurs when information processing exceeds 4.2 bits per second.

Key Findings

  • check_circle Linear correlation between theta-band oscillations and subjective cognitive strain in 94% of participants.
  • check_circle Specific metabolic markers in the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (dlPFC) predictive of decision fatigue.
  • check_circle Evidence of "attentional blink" recovery acceleration when auditory feedback is synchronized with alpha rhythms.

Limitations & Warnings

Sterile Environment Constraints

The fMRI chamber environment lacks the multi-sensory distractions typical of real-world high-frequency environments, potentially over-simplifying neural responses.

Selection Bias

Participants were recruited primarily from academic and professional backgrounds, which may limit the generalizability of cognitive threshold findings.
